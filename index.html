Curated Body‑Weight Exercises

Upper Body

Push‑Ups

Push‑ups strengthen your chest, triceps and shoulders. Begin in a plank position with your hands just outside shoulder width. Keep your body in a straight line, lower yourself until your elbows are near 90 degrees, then press back up. Beginners can modify by placing knees on the ground.

Lower Body

Squats

Squats target your quads, glutes and hamstrings. Stand with feet shoulder‑width apart, toes forward. Brace your core and lower yourself as though sitting into a chair, keeping knees behind toes. Press through your heels to return to standing.

Walking Lunges

Lunges work the quads, hamstrings and glutes while challenging balance. From standing, step forward into a lunge so your front knee bends to 90 degrees and the back knee hovers just above the floor. Push through the front foot to stand and repeat on the other leg while walking forward.

Lateral Lunges

This side‑to‑side variation develops inner‑ and outer‑thigh muscles. Start standing tall with feet together and core engaged. Step out to the side, bending your knee and shifting your weight over the lunging leg. Push the hips back slightly to load the glutes, then return to standing and repeat on the opposite side.

Standing Calf Raises

Focus on the calf muscles by standing with feet hip‑width apart and knees slightly bent. Push through the balls of your feet to lift your heels as high as possible, pausing briefly at the top before lowering back down.

Box Jumps

A plyometric exercise that builds lower‑body power. Face a sturdy box or bench with feet hip‑width apart. Swing your arms back and explosively jump onto the box, extending your hips, knees and ankles. Land softly with both feet and step down carefully. Start with a low platform and increase the height as you gain strength.

Core

Planks

A classic isometric hold that targets the deep abdominal muscles. Place your elbows under your shoulders and extend your legs behind you, balancing on your toes. Keep your body in a straight line and hold this position while maintaining a braced core. Start with 30 seconds and build toward longer holds.

Glute Bridges

Strengthen your hips and hamstrings by lying on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Brace your core, press into the floor and lift your hips until you form a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze your glutes at the top, pause and slowly lower down.

Side Planks

This variation emphasises the obliques. Lie on one side with your elbow under your shoulder. Press your forearm into the floor and raise your hips until your body forms a straight line. Hold while keeping a braced core, then switch sides. Beginners can perform side planks on their knees.

Dead Bugs

A dynamic core exercise that promotes spinal stability. Lie on your back with knees bent at 90 degrees and arms extended toward the ceiling. Flatten your lower back into the floor. Slowly extend your right leg and left arm away from your body without letting your back arch. Return to the starting position and alternate sides.

Cardio & Plyometrics

Burpees

Burpees deliver a full‑body cardiovascular challenge. Start standing, drop into a squat and place your hands on the floor. Jump your feet back into a plank, then return them toward your hands. Finish with an explosive vertical jump, arms overhead. Remove the jump at the end to reduce intensity.

Jumping Jacks

A simple cardio move that gets your heart rate up. Stand with feet together and arms at your sides. Jump your feet out while raising your arms overhead in an arc. Hop back to the starting position with a soft landing and repeat in a rhythmic fashion.
